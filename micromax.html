Why Android’s home look more cluttered than MiUI or iOS?

There are many reasons for the same. 4 of them make it worse -

  • App container
    No app containers means every app is own its own & differ in colours, shapes & sizes. Different shapes & sizes mean different element contacting the user’s eyes & brain. iOS’s uniformity allows users to easily locate & identify the app they are looking for.
  • Widgets & Apps make Home screen
    Widgets also in different shapes & sizes and they are ugly. Nobody designs widgets the way they do their app icons - they are pieces from within the apps. Users put them on the home screen with other apps. A widget could push the app icon either 2 rows down or 1 column right or left - you never know.
  • Randomness
    Apps can be arranged anywhere. One app could be top left and the other could be on the bottom right. Android doesn’t mind. For Android it allows users the freedom to customise but it also then confuses the same user in locating the app when its needed.
  • Auto shortcut
    Every time user downloads an app & Android creates a shortcut on the home screen. If user doesn’t remove the shortcuts auto created - App drawer & home screens would almost have the same number of apps. Most times, users end up keeping apps on the home screen which are not used that often. Overall, Android is not just about a home screen with 24 differently designed icons but multiple home screens where each home screen different from one another - making it difficult to remember & locate where an app is.

    Introducing Home

    Home Page. Each app is different & helps users do different things. We allowed icons to reflect their individuality - tried not to limit them to follow any pattern yet sustained familiarity.

    Also, The apps auto arrange themselves bottom up opposite to topdown approach in iOS. We fixed the app drawer icon to bottom right so the whole screen looks balanced, aligned & clean. Iconography is simple to understand yet modern.

    Most importantly, there is one shape & size so, locating an app is the matter of colour & icon recognition which comes naturally to users.

    App Drawer!

    2 important things we solved from this design.

    App Drawer is where all the apps are. A normal user always thinks once in his Android life why do I have an App Drawer as well as Home screens. I keep apps at both the places. The same user also questions once atleast in his Android life, ‘Is it just me or others also get uneasy finding apps from the app drawer’?

    Actually, getting into app drawer is a struggle of its own kind - overwhelmed by the gorgeous interface - you tend to forget what you came here for at times.

    Problem with App Drawer

    Actually, getting into app drawer is a struggle of its own kind - overwhelmed by the gorgeous interface - you tend to forget what you came here for at times.

    • A grid layout which now comes with a vertical scroll with Android M. People who were atleast able to remember that X app is on the second page lost it with vertical scrolling.
    • Search becomes critical when you have a layout where its difficult to find that one app of 100+ apps you own. ABCD helps but you don’t want to consciously burden your brain with C for CamScanner - you sub conciously remember scan or its colour & typefaces. Until Android M there wasn’t even a search box.
    • There are always a few apps people constantly look for from the app drawer but they don’t want to put them up on the home screen. App Drawer should remember those couple of apps.
